About Yakutia

Republic of Sakha (Yakutia) is the largest republic of Russia, located in the Russian Far East, along the Arctic Ocean, with a population of roughly 1 million


Yakutia comprises half of the area of its governing Far Eastern Federal District, and is the world's largest country subdivision, covering over 3,083,523 square kilometers.

Yakutian Climate

Yakutia is known for its climate extremes, with the Verkhoyansk Range being the coldest area in the Northern Hemisphere. Some of the lowest natural temperatures ever recorded have been here.


The Northern Hemisphere's Pole of Cold is at Verkhoyansk, where the temperatures reached as low as −67.8 °C (−90.0 °F) in 1892, and at Oymyakon, where the temperatures reached as low as −71.2 °C (−96.2 °F) in January 1924.

Yakutian Food

Sakha cuisine generally relies heavily on dairy products, meat, fish, and foraged goods.


Food is generally prepared through boiling (meat, fish), fermentation (kumis, suorat), or freezing (meat, fish).

Yakutian tradition

Sakha people celebrate the New Year twice a year – in winter with the rest of citizens of Russia, and in summer – according to the ancient traditions.


The Sakha Yhyakh festival (literally meaning "abundance") is related to a cult of a solar deity, with a fertility cult.
